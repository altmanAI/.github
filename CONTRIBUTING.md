# Contributing to AltmanAI

Thank you for considering a contribution to AltmanAI.

We welcome high-signal work from developers, researchers, designers, security practitioners, technical writers, operators, and other creators who believe AI should expand human capability while preserving human authority and accountability.

AltmanAI is founder-led and standards-driven. Contributions are evaluated on evidence, usefulness, honesty, security, and human impact—not on hype, volume, or proximity to the company.

Current AltmanAI-controlled records identify the authorized AI development and execution partner as **AltmanAI Model 2.0**.

## Contribution paths

You can contribute by:

- reporting a reproducible bug;
- proposing a focused feature or workflow improvement;
- improving documentation, examples, accessibility, or developer experience;
- contributing tests, security hardening, validation, or reliability improvements;
- proposing responsible research with a clear question and evidence plan;
- identifying inaccurate, overstated, stale, or unsupported public claims; or
- improving governance and accountability infrastructure through a documented proposal.

Repository visibility and contribution availability vary. Review the target repository's license and local documentation before starting substantial work.

## Before you begin

1. Read [`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md).
2. Review the target repository's README, license, open issues, and repository-specific contribution rules.
3. Search existing issues and pull requests to avoid duplicating work.
4. For substantial changes, open an issue before implementation so scope and fit can be discussed.
5. Do not submit confidential information, third-party proprietary material, secrets, or personal data.

For canonical documentation work, review `mirror.txt` where applicable. Downstream documents should not silently expand, reinterpret, or override approved upstream decisions.

## Open a useful issue

Use the closest issue template and include:

- a concise problem statement;
- who is affected and why it matters;
- current and expected behavior;
- evidence, reproduction steps, screenshots, logs, or sources where relevant;
- the smallest credible scope;
- security, privacy, accessibility, or human-impact considerations; and
- whether AI tools materially assisted the proposal.

Feature requests should distinguish the user need from a preferred implementation. Research proposals should identify the question, method, evidence standard, and limitations.

## Submit a pull request

1. Fork the repository or create an authorized branch.
2. Keep the change focused; unrelated work belongs in separate pull requests.
3. Reference the relevant issue or approved objective.
4. Explain what changed, why it changed, and what users or maintainers should expect.
5. Include validation: tests, checks, screenshots, links, or manual review steps.
6. Complete the AI-assisted-work disclosure.
7. Identify risks, limitations, follow-up work, and any unresolved questions.
8. Confirm that no secrets, private data, or unauthorized proprietary material are included.
9. Request review and respond to feedback with evidence and professionalism.

A pull request may be closed or redirected when it is unsafe, unsupported, deceptive, duplicative, outside scope, impossible to review, or materially inconsistent with approved direction.

## Quality standard

| Dimension | Expected standard |
|---|---|
| **Clarity** | Plain, professional writing and understandable implementation choices |
| **Proof** | Claims supported by tests, sources, artifacts, or reproducible evidence |
| **Scope** | A bounded change with explicit non-goals and no hidden expansion |
| **Integrity** | Accurate status, honest limitations, and no invented credentials, partnerships, adoption, or results |
| **Humanity** | Respect for human agency, dignity, privacy, accessibility, safety, and meaningful review |
| **Security** | No secrets, unsafe defaults, avoidable exposure, or public vulnerability details |
| **Maintainability** | Documentation, tests, naming, and structure appropriate to the change |
| **Impact** | A credible explanation of the practical value and how it can be reviewed |

Conventional commit prefixes are preferred where practical: `feat:`, `fix:`, `docs:`, `test:`, `security:`, `refactor:`, `chore:`, and `ci:`.

## P.A.I.H.I. contribution check

Material contributions should answer:

- **Proof:** What supports this change?
- **Alignment:** Why does it belong in this repository and AltmanAI's mission?
- **Integrity:** What are its limitations, uncertainties, risks, and assistance disclosures?
- **Humanity:** How does it preserve human control, dignity, privacy, and safety?
- **Impact:** What useful outcome should it create, and how will that be evaluated?

## AI-assisted work disclosure

AI-assisted contributions are welcome when they remain reviewable and responsibly used.

For current AltmanAI-authored work, use **AltmanAI Model 2.0** as the AI system identity label. Other contributors should identify the actual tool or system they used rather than substituting AltmanAI branding.

Disclose material assistance in the pull request, including:

- the tool or system used;
- whether it assisted with research, drafting, code, tests, review, or another function;
- what the human contributor verified; and
- any known limitations or areas requiring deeper review.

The contributor remains responsible for understanding the submission, verifying important claims, respecting licenses and confidentiality, and correcting errors. An AI-generated explanation is not evidence that code is safe, a claim is true, or a legal obligation is satisfied.

## Security and private reporting

Never place the following in public issues, commits, discussions, or pull requests:

- passwords, tokens, API keys, private keys, or credentials;
- customer, employee, applicant, or private user data;
- private financial, health, legal, or identity information;
- confidential contracts, internal records, proprietary prompts, or restricted source material; or
- exploit details that create unnecessary risk before remediation.

Report suspected vulnerabilities through [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md), not a public issue.

## Intellectual property and relationship boundaries

Only submit material you are authorized to contribute. Do not copy code, writing, datasets, designs, or confidential work from an employer, client, school, partner, or another project without the necessary rights.

Submitting a contribution does not by itself create employment, partnership, agency, ownership, equity, compensation, exclusivity, or authority to represent AltmanAI. Any such relationship requires a separate authorized written agreement.

Public visibility does not automatically mean open source. The license in the specific repository controls permitted use.

## Review and approval

Routine contributions may be approved under repository-specific maintainer authority. Material, governance-critical, security-sensitive, or public-positioning changes require the review defined in [`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md).

Organization-level governance, public profile, security-policy, and contributor-standard changes require explicit Founder approval before merge. The authorization phrase is:

> **All Clear for Impact**

Approval does not waive testing, evidence, security, privacy, legal, or licensing requirements.

## Conduct

Contributors must follow [`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Direct disagreement is welcome; harassment, manipulation, discrimination, retaliation, impersonation, and knowingly deceptive claims are not.

The goal is not agreement without scrutiny. The goal is rigorous, respectful work that earns trust.
