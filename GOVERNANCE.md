# AltmanAI Governance

## Founder-Led Human Accountability for Human-First AI

AltmanAI uses a founder-led governance model designed to keep authority, responsibility, and decision records human and inspectable while allowing qualified people and AI systems to accelerate execution.

This document governs the AltmanAI `.github` organization repository and serves as the default governance baseline for AltmanAI repositories that do not publish a more specific approved policy.

## 1. Governance objectives

AltmanAI governance exists to:

- preserve accountable human authority over material decisions;
- distinguish verified capability from plans, prototypes, and aspirations;
- make materially AI-assisted work transparent;
- protect security, privacy, intellectual property, and stakeholder trust;
- create durable records of important decisions and their evidence;
- welcome high-quality contribution without surrendering standards or misrepresenting contributor status; and
- support practical innovation that improves human capability and control.

## 2. Accountable authority

### Founder & CEO

**Blake Hunter Altman, Founder & Chief Executive Officer of AltmanAI by Altman Family Group LLC**, is the final accountable authority for:

- organization-level governance;
- public company positioning on GitHub;
- material product and repository direction;
- approval of authoritative public records;
- appointments or delegations of repository authority; and
- exceptions to organization-level standards.

Founder-led authority carries an obligation to review evidence, identify material risk, correct inaccurate public claims, and preserve a decision record appropriate to the significance of the change.

### Maintainers

Maintainers operate within delegated repository scope. They may triage issues, review contributions, request evidence, run checks, manage releases, and approve routine changes where repository policy permits.

Maintainer authority does not automatically extend to company-wide public statements, legal commitments, security exceptions, financial commitments, employment or equity promises, or changes to this governance model.

### Contributors

Contributors may propose and implement work through the documented contribution process. Contribution does not by itself create employment, partnership, agency, ownership, equity, or authority to represent AltmanAI.

### AI systems and assistants

AI systems may support research, analysis, drafting, code generation, testing, documentation, classification, and workflow execution when used within an authorized scope.

AI systems do **not** possess independent corporate authority. They may not independently:

- approve or merge material changes;
- publish authoritative company positions;
- enter agreements or make financial commitments;
- assign titles, ownership, equity, employment, or partnership status;
- waive legal, security, privacy, or intellectual-property protections; or
- represent an output as verified when adequate human review and evidence are absent.

The human using or authorizing an AI system remains responsible for reviewing the resulting work at the level appropriate to its risk.

## 3. Decision classes

| Class | Examples | Minimum review |
|---|---|---|
| **Routine** | Typographical fixes, formatting, link maintenance, low-risk dependency housekeeping | Maintainer review and applicable checks |
| **Material** | Public product claims, meaningful feature changes, release positioning, data handling, security controls, licensing, major architecture | Documented evidence, risk review, and authorized human approval |
| **Governance-critical** | Organization profile, governance, security policy, contributor standards, authority model, canonical source-of-truth changes | Founder review and explicit approval before merge or publication |
| **Regulated or high-stakes** | Work affecting legal rights, health, employment, credit, finance, public safety, government decisions, or similarly consequential domains | Specialized review appropriate to the domain; ordinary repository approval alone is insufficient |

A change should be reviewed at the higher class whenever its impact is uncertain.

## 4. Decision process

1. **Open a traceable proposal.** Use an issue or pull request appropriate to the repository.
2. **Define the objective and scope.** State what problem is being solved and what is outside scope.
3. **Attach evidence.** Include sources, tests, screenshots, logs, prototypes, prior decisions, or other relevant artifacts.
4. **State capability accurately.** Label work as proposed, experimental, in development, beta, live, deprecated, or otherwise appropriate.
5. **Disclose material AI assistance.** Identify the tools and the type of assistance when it materially shaped the work.
6. **Review through P.A.I.H.I.** Evaluate proof, alignment, integrity, humanity, and impact.
7. **Complete security and privacy review.** Apply repository-specific controls and escalate sensitive issues privately.
8. **Obtain the required human authorization.** Approval must match the decision class.
9. **Merge and preserve the record.** The final pull request, commit history, and linked artifacts form the public decision trail where disclosure is appropriate.

## 5. P.A.I.H.I. review standard

Material work should answer:

### Proof

- What evidence supports the change or claim?
- Can a reviewer reproduce, inspect, or independently verify the relevant result?
- What remains unverified?

### Alignment

- Does the work match the authorized objective and repository scope?
- Is it consistent with AltmanAI's human-first mission and current approved direction?
- Does it introduce an unstated secondary purpose?

### Integrity

- Are limitations, uncertainty, risk, and AI assistance disclosed honestly?
- Are status, partnerships, credentials, adoption, funding, and performance represented without exaggeration?
- Are conflicts of interest or incentives material to the decision disclosed?

### Humanity

- Does the work preserve meaningful human agency and review?
- Does it protect dignity, privacy, accessibility, safety, and the ability to challenge or correct outputs?
- Could automation create dependency, manipulation, exclusion, or an unreviewable decision path?

### Impact

- What useful outcome should result?
- How will success, failure, and unintended effects be observed?
- What record should be preserved in the repository or Master Ledger?

## 6. Approval control

The explicit approval phrase for governance-critical changes is:

> **All Clear for Impact**

The phrase confirms human authorization by the Founder & CEO when provided in an attributable issue, pull request, or other approved decision record.

It does not waive required testing, security review, privacy obligations, legal review, contractual restrictions, or the need to correct unsupported claims.

## 7. Source-of-truth hierarchy

Unless an approved record states otherwise, the following order applies:

1. Applicable law and binding written agreements;
2. Approved company governance and authorized decisions;
3. This organization-level governance repository;
4. Repository-specific governance, code, tests, releases, and documentation;
5. The [AltmanAI Master Ledger](https://github.com/altmanAI/altmanai-master-ledger) for selected public records and Proof-of-Impact artifacts;
6. Issues, proposals, drafts, discussions, and AI-generated materials that have not yet been approved.

Drafts and generated outputs are not authoritative merely because they are detailed, polished, or publicly visible.

## 8. Security, privacy, and confidential information

No public issue, commit, or pull request may intentionally expose credentials, tokens, private keys, customer data, private financial information, confidential agreements, restricted source material, or exploitable security details.

Security vulnerabilities should be reported through `SECURITY.md`. Contributors must use data minimization and redact sensitive information from evidence whenever possible.

## 9. Conflicts, appeals, and correction

A contributor may challenge a decision by presenting new evidence, identifying a process failure, or showing that the decision conflicts with an approved higher-order record.

AltmanAI may revise or reverse decisions when facts change, evidence improves, risks become clearer, or prior public language proves inaccurate. Corrections should preserve an honest history rather than silently rewriting material records where an amendment or changelog is more appropriate.

## 10. Amendments

Changes to this document are governance-critical. They require:

- a dedicated pull request;
- a clear explanation of the reason and expected impact;
- disclosure of material AI assistance;
- review for conflicts with existing approved records; and
- explicit Founder approval before merge.
